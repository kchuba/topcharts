No.1 - 'Closer' by The Chainsmokers feat. Halsey

Photo by ABC

'Closer' was the top selling U.S. single for a record-tying 13 consecutive weeks in 2016, according to Billboard. The song was also the most-streamed for 12 weeks and most-played on radio for six weeks. For 12 weeks, the song also topped Billboard's Hot 100 list.

Photo by ABC

The Chainsmokers are an EDM DJ duo comprised of Andrew Taggart and Alex Pall. The two had their first hit with 'Selfie' in 2014 and have also landed on the charts in 2016 with 'Roses' and 'Don't Let Me Down,' according to Billboard.

Photo by Justin Higuchi

Halsey, who's real name is Ashley Frangipane, is a 22-year-old pop artist from New York. She released her debut album in 2015 and had another 2016 hit with 'New Americana,' according to Billboard.

Click HERE to hear the song

It was impossible to turn on the radio in 2016 without hearing 'Closer' by The Chainsmokers, featuring Halsey. The song is the undisputed top song of the year, dominating Billboard's Hot 100 chart for 12 weeks, the most of any song this year. It was also the top-selling single for 13 weeks in 2016, tying Billboard's all-time Digital Song Download records. The music video for 'Closer' has over 74 million views and The Chainsmokers and Halsey have performed the song live at both the MTV Video Music Awards and the American Music Awards.

No.2 - 'Heathens' by Twenty One Pilots

Photo by Ian Kennedy

'Heathens' never hit No. 1 on the Billboard Digital Song Sales chart, but was in the top five for 14 weeks. The song was also solid on the Hot 100 charts, peaking at No. 2 and remaining at No. 3 for weeks, as well as No. 2 on most-played on radio, according to Billboard.

Photo by Abby Gillardi

2016 was the year of Twenty One Pilots, as the duo had three top-five songs: 'Heathens,' 'Ride,' and 'Stressed Out,' according to Billboard. With 'Ride' and 'Heathens' on the chart at the same time, Twenty One Pilots became the first alternative band to have two concurrent Top 10 singles.

Photo by Drew de F Fawkes

Twenty One Pilots is a pop/alternative duo who released its third album 'Blurryface' in 2015. The album topped the Billboard 200.

Click HERE to hear the song

Twenty One Pilots became a household name in 2016, with three singles in Billboard's top five and dominating radio time with 'Ride,' 'Heathens' and 'Stressed Out.' Before the pair's rise to fame, they gained attention through social media and online videos they created in their hometown of Columbus, Ohio. Twenty One Pilots went on a world tour in 2016, as well as performed on 'Saturday Night Live' and won the Billboard Music Award for 'Top Rock Artist.'

No.3 - 'Can't Stop the Feeling!' by Justin Timberlake

Photo by Focka

'Can't Stop the Feeling!' spent 10 weeks at the top of the Digital Song Sales chart, only the eighth song in history to do so, according to Billboard. Timberlake's single also spent five weeks on the radio charts and was in the top 10 for streaming.

Photo by Focka

Timberlake's 'Can't Stop the Feeling!' was only the 26th song ever to debut at No. 1 on Billboard's Hot 100 and was the artist's first No. 1 debut. The song also debuted at No. 1 on the Digital Song Sales chart, with 379,000 downloads in the first week.

Photo by J Vettorino

Timberlake has gone from boy-bander to bona fide pop star, with 'Can't Stop the Feeling!' his fourth No. 1 single on the Hot 100 chart. His other hits include 'What Goes Around... Comes Around,' 'My Love' and 'SexyBack,' according to Billboard.

Click HERE to hear the song

Long past his days with *NSYNC, Timberlake has become one of the biggest stars across music, film and TV. It seems that everything he touches turns to gold, and the same can be said of 'Can't Stop the Feeling!,' which he wrote for the film 'Trolls.' The song was No. 1 on the Digital Sales charts for 10 weeks and debuted at No. 1 on Billboard's Hot 100. Timberlake's last album, 'The 20/20 Experience,' was released in two parts in 2013, with five top singles between the two.

No.4 - 'My House' by Flo Rida

Photo by Eva Rinaldi

'My House' became Flo Rida's fourth No. 1 song on the Digital Song Sales chart, joining his singles 'Low,' 'Right Round' and 'Whistle,' according to Billboard. The song was also in the top five on the Hot 100 and in the top 10 for streaming.

Photo by ABC

'My House' was on the top five of the Digital Song Sales chart for 12 weeks. The song was a signle off of Flo Rida's 2015 album of the same name, which also produced hits 'GDFR' and 'I Don't Like It, I Love It,' according to Billboard.

Photo by Eva Rinaldi

Flo Rida, 37, is a South Florida native who got his breakthrough in 2006. He is known for his club hits and frequently works with top producers to create his dance beat, according to Billboard.

Click HERE to hear the song

Over the last 10 years, Flo Rida has consistently appeared on the charts with his dance club hits. Known for classics like 'Low,' 'Whistle,' 'Wild Ones' and 'Right Round,' his latest 'My House' was another hit to add to his list. Flo Rida consistently teams up with top producers like David Guetta and Avicii to create his club tracks. He has released four studio albums and one EP, with plans to release his fifth album 'The Perfect 10' next year, according to Billboard.

No.5 - 'Love Yourself' by Justin Bieber

Photo by Lou Stejskal

Justin Bieber ruled the charts in January 2016, taking over the top two spots on Billboard's Hot 100 chart with his singles 'Love Yourself' and 'Sorry.' The former took the top spot on the Digital Song Sales chart for three weeks in a row.

Photo by Adam Sundana

'Lose Yourself,' off of Bieber's 2015 album 'Purpose,' is his third single to top Billboard's Hot 100 chart, alongside 'Sorry' and 'What Do You Mean?' His other top five songs include 'Boyfriend,' 'Beauty and a Beat' and 'Baby.'

Photo by ABC

Bieber, 22, found fame in 2009 after his YouTube videos became popular online. Usher signed Bieber to a recording contract and has worked with him across his four albums, according to Billboard.

Click HERE to hear the song

Justin Bieber may be known just as much for his antics as for his musical success, but his 2015 album 'Purpose' introduced a more serious side for the singer. Along with topping the charts with 'Love Yourself,' he also had hits with 'Sorry,' 'What Do You Mean??' and 'Where Are U Now' off of the album. 'Love Yourself' spent three weeks on top of the Digital Song Sales chart, along with two weeks as No. 1 on Billboard's Hot 100 and topped the charts for radio play.
